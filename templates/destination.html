Ambuluwawa

3 Reviews

In the central highlands of Sri Lanka, Ambuluwawa is a wildlife complex and Sri Lanka's first multi-religious sanctuary. The Ambuluwawa Trigonometrical Station, in Gampola, consists of large winding tower resembling a stylized Buddhist stupa, a Hindu kovil, a Muslim mosque and a Christian Church. Ambuluwawa is a short hike that provides panoramic views of the surrounding mountains, woods, rivers, and cities. Ambuluwawa lies roughly 1,965 feet above the Gampola town level. Ambuluwawa Mountain rises to a height of around 3560 meters above sea level. The tower rises to a height of around 48 meters. The mountain road runs along the serpentine surrounded by mountain landscapes, cliffs and unique plants, which cannot be found anywhere else. Interesting places to visit include three ponds, a sacred Bodhi tree, water Park, stone Park and garden of herbs.
